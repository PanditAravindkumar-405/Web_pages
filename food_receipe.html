Gulab-Jamun

Ingredients

  • Khoya
  • Paneer
  • Maida
  • Baking soda
  • Milk
  • Ghee or oil
  • Sugar

Receipe

To make Gulab Jamun, start by preparing sugar syrup with sugar, water, cardamom, and optional saffron and rose water. Simmer until slightly thickened and set aside. For the dough, mix khoya, flour, and baking soda, kneading with milk until smooth. Shape the dough into small, crack-free balls. Heat oil or ghee on medium-low and fry the balls until golden brown, stirring gently for even cooking. Drain the fried balls and soak them in the warm sugar syrup for at least an hour. Serve warm or at room temperature, garnished with nuts or saffron, for a delightful, sweet treat.



Milk-Barfi

Ingredients

  1. Khoya
  2. Milk powder
  3. Sugar
  4. Milk
  5. Ghee (clarified butter)
  6. Cardamom powder
  7. Chopped nuts (optional)
  8. Saffron strands (optional)

Receipe

To make a simple milk-based recipe, heat milk in a saucepan over medium heat, stirring occasionally to prevent burning. Add sugar and a pinch of cardamom powder for flavor. Bring the milk to a gentle boil, then reduce the heat and simmer until it thickens slightly. You can add saffron strands for a rich aroma and color. For a creamy texture, stir in a little ghee. Once the milk reaches your desired consistency, remove from heat. Pour into a serving dish and garnish with chopped nuts. Serve warm or chilled for a delicious, comforting treat.



Ice-Cream

Ingredients

  1. Khoya
  2. Heavy cream
  3. Whole milk
  4. Sugar
  5. Vanilla extract (or any other flavoring)
  6. Optional mix-ins (chocolate chips, fruit, nuts, etc.)

Receipe

To make ice cream, start by whisking together heavy cream, whole milk, sugar, and vanilla extract in a bowl until the sugar dissolves. Pour the mixture into an ice cream maker and churn according to the manufacturer's instructions until it reaches a soft, creamy consistency. If desired, add mix-ins like chocolate chips, fruit, or nuts during the last few minutes of churning. Once done, transfer the ice cream to an airtight container and freeze for a few hours to firm up. Scoop and serve your homemade ice cream, enjoying its rich, creamy flavor and customizable texture.



Rasgulla

Ingredients

  1. Khoya
  2. Milk
  3. Lemon juice or vinegar
  4. Sugar
  5. Water
  6. Cardamom powder (optional)
  7. Rose water (optional)
  8. Saffron strands (optional)

Receipe

To make Rasgulla, boil milk and add lemon juice or vinegar to curdle it. Strain the curds through a muslin cloth, rinse with cold water, and squeeze out excess moisture to form chenna. Knead the chenna until smooth and soft, then shape it into small balls. In a separate pot, prepare sugar syrup by boiling sugar and water, adding cardamom powder for flavor. Gently drop the chenna balls into the boiling syrup and cook for 15-20 minutes until they double in size. Remove from heat and let the Rasgullas soak in the syrup. Serve chilled, garnished with saffron or rose water.
